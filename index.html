Rejection Rate of Refugees By Each Continent



Even with the large influx of refugees and asylum seekers, there are only a handful of countries that bear almost all the responsibility.

According to the bump chart Oceania is notorious for consistently having the highest average rejection rate (38.26% in 2021) of refugees throughout 2010 - 2021, even though it receives the least amount of refugee applications. Second to this is Europe (29.71% in 2021).

Acceptance Rate of Refugees By Nations



While Europe has one of the highest refugee rejection rates, it also has the highest acceptance rate amongst other continents. Out of the European nations, France, Italy and Spain provide complementary protection to many refugees, and the one leading the charge from 2016 to 2021 being Germany.

Germany became more accepting of refugees and asylum seekers after 2015. They have taken a pivotal shift in their culture to welcome refugees and assist them getting acclimated to German society. This has helped solve the demographic and economic problems face in the country.

Where Do Most Externally Displaced People Come From?




69% of Asylum Seekers and Refugees in 2021 originate from just five countries: Syria, Venezuela, Afghanistan, South Sudan and Myanmar. What is prevalent in all of these countries is the currently ongoing warfare within the nations forcing millions of citizens to leave their homes to protect themselves.
